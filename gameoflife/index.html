What is this?

Game of Life is a simulation made up of cells that can be either dead or alive. The simulation has very simple rules: if a cell is dead, and has exactly 3 alive neighbours, it will be alive the next timestep. A cell with fewer than 2 or more than 3 alive neighbours will die. Otherwise a cell stays the same. The fascination of the Game of Life is that from such simple rules on an individual cell level spawns incredibly complex behaviour on a macro scale - in fact, the Game of Life is Turing complete.

How to play?

This implementation has two panels, with buttons in the middle of them. The upper panel shows the game itself, as the simulation plays out. The "play" and "pause" buttons start and stop the simulation. The "load" button loads the board to the simulation. This board can be inputted into the lower panels text input. Zeroes represent dead cells and ones represent alive cells. The "delay" slider controls the delay between each timestep, so in practice the speed of the simulation.

Have fun!
